Our Research Programs

Combining molecular biology, mouse models, and human tissue samples to understand and ultimately treat cardiac arrhythmias and cardiomyopathies.

Atrial Fibrillation & Inflammasome Signaling

Our group was the first to establish a causal role for inflammasome signaling in atrial fibrillation. We map mechanistic links between AF and risk factors including clonal hematopoiesis, liver disease, and aging.

Yao et al., Circulation 2018 →

FKBP5 in Atrial Cardiomyopathy

We identified FK506-binding protein 5 (FKBP5) as a novel molecular contributor to atrial cardiomyopathy. Studies delineate its role across cardiac cell types and its involvement in fibrotic remodeling.

Read Publication →

Cardiac Conduction Disorders

In collaboration with Dr. Mihail Chelu at BCM, we use mouse models and human biopsy samples to uncover molecular mechanisms underlying sinoatrial node dysfunction and atrioventricular block.

TANGO2-Deficiency Disorder

TDD is caused by TANGO2 gene mutations and leads to life-threatening arrhythmias. We aim to understand TANGO2 function in cardiomyocytes using a knockout mouse model to elucidate the underlying mechanisms.
